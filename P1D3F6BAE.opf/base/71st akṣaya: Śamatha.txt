pa’i lam na źi gnas rab tu sbyor ba ste, de’i dbaṅ du byas nas de la źi gnas mi zad pa gaṅ
źe na źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so – "As for the bodhisattva progressing in the practice
of collecting (saṃkṣepayogapratipanna), he is the one practising peaceful meditation on
the way of practice, vision and cultivation (yaḥ prayogadarśanabhāvanāmārgeṣu śamathaprayuktaḥ);
with reference to him (tam ādhipatiṃ kṛtvā) it is said: ...źi gnas mi zad pa yaṅ
źi gnas kyi tshogs mi zad pa daṅ źi gnas ñid mi zad pa ste, rnam pa gñis su rig par bya’o,
de la gaṅ sems źi ba daṅ źes bya ba nas brtsams te btaṅ sñoms sgom źes bya ba yan chad ni
źi gnas kyi tshogs so, bsam gtan daṅ po źes bya ba nas brtsams te dgu po ’di dag ni źi gnas
źes bya’o źes bya ba yan chad kyis źi gnas ñid bstan to – "As for the imperishability of
peaceful meditation (śamathākṣayatā): both the accumulations for peaceful meditation (śamathasaṃbhāra)
and peaceful meditation itself (śamatha eva) are imperishable (akṣaya) ..."
