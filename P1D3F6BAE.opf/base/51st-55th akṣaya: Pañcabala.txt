rnams kyis mi tshugs pa la stobs źes bya’o – "These five beginning with faith are also
called powers (bala). When treated as abilities (indriya) they only have mastery (ādhipatya)
in realizing (abhimukhīkaraṇa) and fulfilling (abhinirhāra) the moments of existence in
which one should believe, etc., they are not such that they sweep away the contraries (vipakṣanirlekha).
When treated as powers (bala), however, faith and the others, when cultivated
(prabhāvita) and purified (viśodhita), attain strength (sthāmabhūta) and are powers (bala)
so as to sweep away their contraries, lack of faith, etc. (āśraddhyādivipakṣanirlekha)".Abhidharmasamuccaya
p. 7410-11 treats the distinction between indriya and bala as follows:
yathā pañcendriyāṇi tathā pañcabalāni / eṣāṃ viśeṣaḥ tair vipakṣāntarāyanirllekho ’navamṛdyateti

// phale tu viśeṣaḥ / tathā hy eṣāṃ tac ca yathoktam – āśraddhyādivipakṣanirlekhaś cādhika,
ity ata evaiṣāṃ tulyānām ālaṃbanasvabhāvādikānām apy anavamṛdyatārthaviśeṣeṇa bodhipakṣāntaratvam
//The āśraddhyādivipakṣanirlekhaś cādhika in the bhāṣya is said to be a quotation,
but it can hardly be a quotation from our ṭīkā, which quotes the Abhidharmasamuccayabhāṣya
elsewhere. The adhika is not found in ṭ, which also suggests a common source for the
sentence. Cf. the introduction on the quotations in ṭ and on the author of ṭ.de mi zad pa
yaṅ de dag gis dad par bya ba’i chos rnams mi zad pas de la dad par byed pa mi zad pa’am,
